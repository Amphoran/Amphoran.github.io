About Us
Grover Cleveland, the 22nd and 24th President of the United States, had two non-consecutive terms in office. During his first term (1885-1889), he focused on fiscal conservatism, civil service reform, and tariff reduction. He supported lower tariffs and implemented the Pendleton Civil Service Act to combat corruption. However, he faced criticism for his handling of labor strikes and was unable to achieve significant tariff reform due to opposition within his party.
